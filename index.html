I. INTRODUCTION

UKM  Cyber  Security   ITS  (Unit Kegiatan  Mahasiswa  Cyber Security   Institut
Teknologi Sepuluh Nopember) is a student activity unit at the Institut Teknologi
Sepuluh Nopember (ITS)  in Surabaya, Indonesia,  dedicated  to   the  field   of
cybersecurity. Stems within the  campus community  as early as  2019 from Heroes
Cyber Security, this UKM serves  as a primary hub for students passionate  about
information security, ethical hacking, and digital defense.

The   unit actively bridges  the  gap  between  academic  knowledge and industry
practice, providing a platform for students  to develop practical skills through
various  activities. Members engage  in  a  range of cybersecurity  disciplines,
including  web exploitation, binary exploitation, and reverse  engineering.  The
UKM is also a key  collaborator  in   national and international events, such as
CyberHack in 2026,  a collaborative  hackathon with  Xtremax Indonesia   and AWS
Indonesia that challenged students to apply AI and software engineering to solve
real-world industry problems.

II. PROGRAMS

UKM Cyber Security ITS conducts its educational operations into two  categories:
technical   and  managerial.   Within  technical cybersecurity   education,  The
Community conducts Weekly Training and MaBar CTF, which  are held on alternating
weeks.  Meanwhile,  The   Community conducts   several  packages  of  managerial
educational programs codenamed Project BLOOM.

Weekly Training consists of lectures, hands-on practices, and mentoring covering
six CTF categories:  web exploitation,  digital forensics, cryptography, reverse
engineering,    binary  exploitation, and blockchain.  Weekly Training  is often
conducted online but sometimes offline,   either on or off the ITS  campus.  The
average duration of an  event is about 2 hours. Participants are equally able to
ask questions  and   discuss  problems  they  might  encounter throughout  their
learning phases.

MaBar CTF leverages  the momentum of global CTF events,  which mostly take place
on weekends.  Occasionally,  national contests take priority  over international
ones due to the widespread use of large  language models  in international CTFs.
In  this event, undergraduate   students incrementally practice  the skills they
have learned while simultaneously developing their collaboration skills.

Project BLOOM  deserves  its own section due  to its    specialty, novelty,  and
volatility in its current development phase.

III. TRANSPARENCY

The Community requires a  hybrid high-trust and zero-trust architecture in order
to simulate high-quality community governance. The Community values meritocracy,
technocracy, and transparency.  Thus,  a   transparency dashboard must be   made
available for access by everyone. This transparency   model utilizes centralized
blockchain technology viewable by  anyone,   even outside of  The Community. The
data is made accessible for research and evaluation purposes, with the hope that
The Community's initiative will  inspire other communities to employ and improve
upon the  same   base  system.   Our   transparency    page is   available    at
https://ucs.or.id/transparency.
